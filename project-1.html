The Design Challenge

Revamp and modernize their existing website, maintaining their non-profit identity while improving navigation, organizing information, and facilitating easy editing for board members.

Researching the Problem

Before initiating our redesign, we conducted research through surveys to pinpoint the predominant challenges encountered by existing users necessitating resolution. Our central objective was to elevate community engagement, striving to strike a balance between a polished redesign and familiarity for our users, thus upholding accessibility as a paramount concern. This data guided us in prioritizing features and determining the most effective means to represent their community.

The Problem and Our Hypothesis

IHH site users seek a sense of community, but some lack engagement due to geographical constraints. Users will be able to feel more connected to the IHH community by being involved in a variety of local events and volunteer opportunities.

Gathering Inspiration From Competitors

Before delving into our solutions, we researched website designs of various non-profit organizations, ranging from well-known entities to local nonprofits in our vicinity. Our aim was to identify successful strategies and opportunities for improvement to inform our prototype development.

Exploring Possible Solutions

Considering our research findings, community events were paramount for our users, serving as a vital connection point during their challenges. Our prototypes prioritized seamless information sharing, effortless event registration, and clear event calendars for effective planning.

A/B Testing on Desktop

We did the same test on desktop to see if the users would prefer the same things. What we uncovered was that for desktop, the users preferences were totally opposite. Many of our users stated that the pop up window for desktop was disorientating and distracting. They preferred to have a page dedicated to filling out their information.

Final Design Decisions

Based on our A/B testing results, we opted to retain the pop-up form for mobile and maintain the in-page sign-up for desktop. Additionally, we introduced a dedicated button to view all events on both platforms, following positive feedback from users.

Matching UI to Branding

Intermountain Healing Hearts supplied their brand colors and assets used for marketing events, social gatherings, and logos. They requested our design team to select new fonts aligning with their website's refresh, utilizing provided materials to maintain brand consistency.
