Redesiging Outdated Webpages for a Non-profit

The Client's Story

Prior to commencing our redesign, we sought to identify the prevalent issues faced by current users that required attention. Our primary focus was on enhancing community engagement, and we aimed to ensure that our redesign appeared refined yet familiar to our users, maintaining accessibility as a key priority. This data guided us in prioritizing features and determining the most effective means to represent their community.

Client Constraints

A significant number of individuals become aware of congenital heart disease only after their child's birth. According to testimonials from current users, many engage in extensive research via their phones during the journey home from the hospital. Recognizing that the majority of our users access IHH's website via mobile devices, we embraced a mobile-first approach to redesigning the website.

The Design Challenge

Revamp and modernize their existing website, maintaining their non-profit identity while improving navigation, organizing information, and facilitating easy editing for board members.

Researching the Problem

Before initiating our redesign, we conducted research through surveys to pinpoint the predominant challenges encountered by existing users necessitating resolution. Our central objective was to elevate community engagement, striving to strike a balance between a polished redesign and familiarity for our users, thus upholding accessibility as a paramount concern. This data guided us in prioritizing features and determining the most effective means to represent their community.
